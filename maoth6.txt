# 🎙️ "SIX MONTHS THAT CHANGED EVERYTHING"
## A News Script on AI — January to June 2026
### Estimated Read Time: 7–8 minutes | Can be trimmed to 5 min (see cut notes)

---

> **ANCHOR NOTE:** This is written as a single-anchor broadcast narrative. Tone: urgent, cinematic, authoritative — like a documentary voiceover crossed with breaking news. Pause marks [ ] indicate breath beats.

## COLD OPEN

*[Low music. Slow build.]*

"Six months ago, we stood at the edge of something.

We could feel it — in the hum of data centers, in the nervous chatter of boardrooms, in the quiet panic of governments trying to regulate something that was already moving faster than the law.

We just didn't know *how big* the wave would get.

Now we do.

[ ]

This is six months in Artificial Intelligence. January to June, 2026. Not a tech story. Not a business story.

A *history* story.

Let's go back to the beginning."

---

## ACT I — JANUARY: THE YEAR WAKES UP HUNGRY

*[Music shifts — purposeful, driving]*

"January, 2026. The industry barely paused for New Year's.

The tone was set immediately: this would be a year not of *promise* — but of *proof*. The analysts said it clearly — AI enthusiasm was giving way to scrutiny. The question wasn't *can it?* anymore. The question was *will it pay?*

But before anyone could answer that… the machines started shipping.

Google opened the year announcing that **Personal Intelligence** was coming to Gemini — AI that learns your Gmail, your Photos, your calendar — and responds like it's known you for years. Personalized AI. For everyone. Right in your pocket.

Meanwhile, at CES in Las Vegas, NVIDIA pulled back the curtain on **Vera Rubin** — its next-generation AI chip platform, built to handle *trillion-parameter models*. [ ] Trillion. Not billion. The hardware arms race had reached a new altitude.

And in the pharmaceutical world, Eli Lilly quietly flipped a switch on **LillyPod** — the most powerful AI supercomputer in the history of medicine. Where traditional labs test two thousand molecular hypotheses a year? LillyPod can simulate *billions*. In parallel. AI wasn't just writing code anymore. It was hunting for cures.

January said: *We mean business.*"

---

## ACT II — FEBRUARY: THE ACCUSATION

*[Music drops slightly. Tension.]*

"February brought something darker.

The model releases kept coming — Claude Opus 4.6, Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.3 Codex, Gemini 3.1 Pro — twelve major AI updates in a single month. The pace was dizzying. Analysts tracked over *255 model releases* across organizations in Q1 alone.

But February's biggest story wasn't a model.

It was an accusation.

**Anthropic publicly alleged that DeepSeek** — China's breakout AI star — had used thousands of fraudulent accounts to generate *millions* of conversations with Claude, harvesting that data to train its own models. Stealing intelligence from intelligence. [ ]

The White House weighed in. The tech CEOs weighed in. And DeepSeek — the scrappy Chinese upstart that had rattled Silicon Valley just a year before by delivering near-frontier performance for a fraction of the cost — was suddenly in the center of a geopolitical storm.

This wasn't just a copyright dispute. This was the US-China AI rivalry coming to a boil.

And it was only February."

---

## ACT III — MARCH: THE GOLD RUSH

*[Music picks up — faster, more competitive]*

"March. And the gold rush was on.

The model releases weren't slowing — they were *accelerating*. Every two to three weeks, a new flagship. GPT-5.4 from OpenAI. DeepSeek V4 emerging on the horizon, deliberately timed to coincide with China's political season — the *Two Sessions* — a symbolic declaration that China's AI ambitions were now state policy.

And here's what nobody was saying out loud, but everyone was feeling: **the rules of the game had changed.**

You couldn't pick one AI model anymore and commit. The era of model *loyalty* was dead. The new era of model *routing* had begun — developers juggling GPT-5.4, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek simultaneously, assigning each task to the best tool for the job.

AI had become infrastructure. Like electricity. You don't ask which power plant lit your lamp.

Meanwhile, something else was happening underground. A protocol called **MCP** — Anthropic's Model Context Protocol — crossed *97 million installs*. The plumbing of agentic AI was quietly becoming universal. The world was being wired for a future where AI agents don't just answer questions — they *take action.*

March said: the frontier has no finish line."

---

## ACT IV — APRIL: THE WEEK SILICON VALLEY LOST ITS MIND

*[Music peaks — dramatic, intense]*

"April 23rd, 2026. Mark the date.

Because on that single day — *one single Thursday* — OpenAI launched **GPT-5.5**. Its most powerful model yet. Sixty percent fewer hallucinations than its predecessor. Benchmark scores that made the previous generation look quaint.

[ ]

And then — less than *twenty-four hours later* — DeepSeek dropped **V4**. A *trillion-parameter* open-source model, running on Huawei's domestic chips, priced at fourteen *cents* per million tokens. Not dollars. *Cents.*

The message was unmistakable: *China doesn't need your Nvidia chips anymore.*

But that wasn't even the biggest news.

The same week, **Meta announced eight thousand layoffs** — ten percent of its workforce — while simultaneously revealing it would *double* its AI spending to a hundred and fifteen billion dollars in 2026. They weren't retreating. They were *reloading.*

And if that wasn't enough drama for one week — Samsung confirmed it was targeting **800 million devices** powered by Google's Gemini AI by year end. Eight hundred million. That's nearly one-tenth of *all humans alive.*

[ ]

April wasn't a news cycle. April was a *declaration of war*. Every major player, in every country, showing their hand at once.

The stakes had never been higher."

---

## ACT V — MAY/JUNE PART I: THE MONEY RECKONS

*[Music shifts — grand, sweeping, historical weight]*

"And then came the money.

May 20th, 2026: **SpaceX filed its S-1** with the SEC — the first domino in what would become the most consequential IPO wave since the internet era. Three companies. Three trillion-dollar ambitions. All going public within months of each other.

On June 1st, **Anthropic officially filed for its IPO** — with a valuation sitting at *nine hundred and sixty-five billion dollars*. Nearly a trillion. For a company that didn't exist six years ago. Its run-rate revenue had crossed *forty-seven billion dollars* in May alone.

OpenAI was preparing its own confidential filing. Together, the three listings — SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic — could represent *three point seven trillion dollars* in combined market cap.

For context: that's larger than the GDP of France.

[ ]

And Elon Musk? He wasn't done. Days after SpaceX went public, he confirmed a **sixty-billion-dollar acquisition of Cursor** — the beloved AI coding tool — putting SpaceX in direct competition with Anthropic's Claude Code and OpenAI's Codex.

The AI industry wasn't just changing the world anymore. It was eating *itself* — and getting bigger every bite."

---

## ACT VI — JUNE: THE BAN, THE HEIST, THE PHONE IN YOUR POCKET

*[Music becomes tense, then triumphant]*

"June. And three stories collided at once.

First: the ban.

On June 12th, the US government issued an export control directive — and Anthropic was forced to pull **Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5** offline. Globally. Overnight. Users woke up to error messages. Developers scrambled. The AI that powered their workflows had simply... vanished.

[ ]

The reason? The government demanded *perfect jailbreak resistance*. A bar that, by Anthropic's own admission, no AI company can currently meet. It was like demanding a bank exist without any possibility of robbery — and closing it when it couldn't guarantee that.

The models came back online around June 18th. But the message lingered: *governments can now switch off frontier AI like a light.*

The geopolitical stakes of this technology had become viscerally, uncomfortably real.

[ ]

Second: the heist.

**Noam Shazeer** — co-author of the 2017 paper *'Attention Is All You Need'*, the foundational text of modern AI — announced he was leaving Google DeepMind to join OpenAI. The man who invented the Transformer architecture. The intellectual cornerstone of *every AI model in existence*.

Google had paid *two point seven billion dollars* to bring him back from his own startup just 22 months earlier.

He lasted less than two years.

Sam Altman called it a hire he had 'wanted since the very beginning.' Google's internal reaction was reportedly described as *shell-shocked.*

[ ]

And third — the moment AI came to *your* phone.

At WWDC, Apple unveiled **Siri AI** — a complete reinvention of its assistant, powered by Google's Gemini, with a new standalone app, deep system integration, and — for the first time — the ability for users to choose their own AI. Claude. Gemini. ChatGPT. All living inside your iPhone, accessible with a swipe.

Tim Cook wiped a tear during the keynote. His last WWDC. After 25 years of building the device in your pocket — he handed it to an AI.

[ ]

Meanwhile, ChatGPT's market share fell below fifty percent for the first time in history. The AI throne now has three claimants. And the contest — for your time, your data, your trust — has only just begun."

*[Music fades to near-silence. Anchor speaks slowly, deliberately.]*

"Six months.

Cures being simulated at the speed of light. Models being banned by governments. Trillion-dollar companies materializing from code and compute. A co-author of the most important AI paper ever written, changing sides.

And Siri — the assistant we made fun of for ten years — finally... waking up.

Here's what these six months taught us:

AI is no longer a *technology* story. It is an *everything* story. It is a healthcare story, a national security story, a stock market story, a philosophy story. It is the story of what humans decide to build — and who controls what gets built.

[ ]

We are not watching the future arrive.

We are *in it*.

And the next six months? If history is any guide...

...we haven't seen anything yet.
